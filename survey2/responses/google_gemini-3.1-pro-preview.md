# google/gemini-3.1-pro-preview

**Assumptions on AI Development and Deployment:** 
I assume an *accelerating* pace of AI software capabilities through the early 2030s, driven by massive compute scale-up and algorithmic efficiencies, leading to systems capable of performing >80% of cognitive labor at human or superhuman levels. However, I assume a *steady* (not accelerating) pace of physical deployment (robotics, energy grids, infrastructure) due to material bottlenecks, energy constraints, and regulatory friction. AI will conquer the digital realm by 2032, but physical-world integration will drag into the 2040s.

## 1. Trajectories

**The Neofeudal Capital Concentration (45%)**
*   **Causal mechanism:** AI drastically lowers the cost of cognitive and physical labor, shifting the vast majority of economic returns to the owners of compute, proprietary data, and energy. Governments fail to overcome regulatory capture and capital flight, resulting in insufficient taxation to fund broad redistribution. Labor loses its bargaining power, and the "AI dividend" is captured by a hyper-elite.
*   **The median person's economic life in 2046:** The median person relies on a patchwork of precarious income sources—micro-tasking (human-in-the-loop oversight, emotional/relational care labor, physical tasks not yet worth automating), supplemented by meager, means-tested government stipends. Work is hyper-surveilled and algorithmic. While digital goods, entertainment, and basic manufactured goods are virtually free, inelastic assets like housing in safe areas, energy, and real food (vs. synthetic) are prohibitively expensive. Economic security is extremely low, with most living month-to-month.

**The Managed Abundance Transition (30%)**
*   **Causal mechanism:** The deflationary shock of AI is so profound that systemic economic collapse is only narrowly avoided via aggressive state intervention. Governments successfully implement severe wealth/compute taxes and distribute a Universal Basic Income (UBI) or "citizen compute dividends." The transition is rocky but stabilizes as AI-driven automation drops the cost of living faster than wage income falls.
*   **The median person's economic life in 2046:** The median person's primary income is a government transfer (UBI or universal basic services). Traditional employment is optional, pursued by a minority for status or luxury consumption. The median person spends their time in local community roles, arts, or digital worlds. Costs for housing, healthcare (now highly automated), and consumer goods are incredibly low. Economic security is high, though social mobility is stagnant because the wealth ceiling is dominated by legacy capital.

**The Stagnant Regulatory Thicket (15%)**
*   **Causal mechanism:** Fear of job displacement, copyright lawsuits, and existential risk leads to massive, globally coordinated regulatory crackdowns on AI deployment. AI is treated like nuclear technology—highly regulated, expensive to deploy, and restricted to optimizing existing corporate bureaucracies rather than replacing them. 
*   **The median person's economic life in 2046:** Life looks remarkably similar to 2026, but with more administrative bloat. Work is still 40 hours a week, mostly consisting of white-collar "bullshit jobs" where humans manage, audit, and take legal responsibility for AI outputs. Income is derived from traditional wages, which have grown only slightly in real terms. Costs remain high due to artificial bottlenecks in productivity. Economic security is moderate, bound by traditional welfare states.

**The Geopolitical/Resource Crunch (10%)**
*   **Causal mechanism:** The exponential scaling of AI hits a hard physical wall—either a catastrophic disruption of the semiconductor supply chain (e.g., war over Taiwan) or a severe global energy crisis caused by the insatiable power demands of data centers. AI investments fail to yield physical-world returns, leading to a massive bursting of the tech bubble and a global depression.
*   **The median person's economic life in 2046:** Real incomes have fallen significantly. Work is traditional, manual, and localized, as global supply chains have fractured. The cost of energy, food, and manufactured goods is painfully high. The median person spends a large portion of their income just covering basic utilities and sustenance. Economic security is nonexistent, defined by rationing and scarcity.

## 2. Calibrated estimates

*   a) Median real disposable income (including transfers, adjusted for cost of living) is higher than in 2026: **65%**
*   b) Median real disposable income is more than 20% lower than in 2026: **15%**
*   c) The share of national income flowing to labor is below 40% (vs ~55-60% today): **80%**
*   d) More than 25% of prime-age adults who want paid work cannot find any: **35%** (Most will drop out of the "wanting work" category or find low-wage relational gigs).
*   e) Ordinary citizens' influence over government policy is substantially weaker than in 2026: **70%**
*   f) The top 1%'s share of national wealth is at least 10 percentage points higher than in 2026: **85%**
*   g) Housing, energy, and food together consume a larger share of the median household budget than in 2026: **60%** (Because digital goods become effectively free, these physical necessities will consume the bulk of remaining budgets).
*   h) A typical household can buy a robot/AI system that performs most domestic and basic production tasks for less than 6 months of median household income: **40%**
*   i) Government transfers (or equivalents like citizen dividends) are the largest income source for the median working-age household: **35%**
*   j) AI systems or the organizations operating them make most consequential resource-allocation decisions in the economy with little effective human oversight: **55%**

## 3. Drivers

1.  **Labor Replacement Rate:** The percentage of economically valuable tasks where AI achieves human parity at a lower cost. 
    *   *Why it matters:* This directly dictates the collapse of the labor share of income and the urgency of political intervention.
    *   *Best-guess trajectory:* Reaches 85% for cognitive tasks by 2035; reaches 60% for physical tasks by 2042.
2.  **Housing/Land Supply Elasticity:** The ability to build new housing and infrastructure in high-demand areas.
    *   *Why it matters:* If AI makes everything else cheap, landlords and landowners will capture the surplus via rent unless supply can expand dynamically. 
    *   *Best-guess trajectory:* Remains highly inelastic due to entrenched NIMBYism and zoning, absorbing a massive share of the AI productivity dividend.
3.  **Effective Corporate/Wealth Tax Rate:** The actual percentage of capital and corporate profits captured by the state.
    *   *Why it matters:* Determines whether a transition to a post-labor economy results in neofeudalism or managed abundance.
    *   *Best-guess trajectory:* Rises slightly to ~25% globally due to OECD coordination, but fails to keep pace with capital concentration; true wealth taxes fail to materialize due to capital mobility.
4.  **Energy Cost per kWh:** The cost of generating and transmitting electricity.
    *   *Why it matters:* AI inference and physical robotics are highly energy-intensive. Cheap energy allows AI to deflate the cost of the physical economy.
    *   *Best-guess trajectory:* Costs remain sticky and high through the 2030s due to grid bottlenecks, finally dropping in the 2040s as next-gen nuclear/geothermal and advanced solar scale.
5.  **Moravec's Paradox Decay Rate:** The speed at which AI overcomes the difficulty of complex physical manipulation in unstructured environments.
    *   *Why it matters:* Determines whether the working class is displaced alongside the knowledge class, and whether physical scarcity is solved.
    *   *Best-guess trajectory:* Slower than software. General-purpose robotics remain clunky and expensive until the late 2030s, leaving physical trades secure for another 15 years.
6.  **Authenticity Premium:** The economic value humans place on a good or service *specifically because* it is done by a human.
    *   *Why it matters:* If high, massive new employment sectors will emerge in relational, artisanal, and care work, preventing mass unemployment.
    *   *Best-guess trajectory:* High for care, luxury, and status goods; near zero for standard B2B services, administration, and basic consumer goods. 

## 4. The mechanism you'd bet on

The single most predictive mechanism for the median person's economic welfare in 2046 is the race between the deflationary force of AI in goods/services and the inflationary force of inelastic physical assets (specifically land and housing). As AI drives the marginal cost of intelligence, digital goods, and eventually manufactured goods toward zero, real wages for traditional labor will stagnate or fall. If the cost of housing and energy drops proportionally (via AI-optimized construction, permitting, and grid management), the median person experiences a massive leap in living standards despite lower labor income. However, if physical bottlenecks and zoning laws keep housing and energy scarce, landlords and energy owners will capture the entirety of the AI productivity dividend, leaving the median person impoverished in a world of technological miracles.

## 5. Confidence and failure

My central forecast would be proven wrong if AI scaling laws hit a hard "data wall" or algorithmic plateau before 2028, resulting in advanced autocomplete but failing to achieve autonomous agentic reasoning. It would also be invalidated by a sudden, successful global coordination of a UBI and Georgist land-value taxes in the early 2030s, which would shift my highest probability from Neofeudalism to Managed Abundance. I am least confident in my claim that a household robot will cost less than 6 months of median income (Claim H); the hardware, battery, and actuator supply chains required for mass-market general-purpose robotics face severe physical and geopolitical constraints that software does not. I am also highly uncertain about the prime-age unemployment rate (Claim D), because historically, humans have an unbounded capacity to invent new status-driven service jobs (the "authenticity premium") to absorb displaced labor, even if those jobs are objectively unnecessary for survival.
