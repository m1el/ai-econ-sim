# The demographic undertow: how the aging population interacts with all five futures

*Companion to the [five futures](NARRATIVES.md) and the [objections](OBJECTIONS.md). Developed economies are aging fast — the old-age dependency ratio goes from ~30 retirees per 100 working-age adults today to ~45–50 by 2046. That trend was set in motion decades before AI mattered, and it collides with every scenario.*

**The frame that makes this simple:** a pensioner already lives the post-labor life. No wage; income from transfers and assets; welfare dependent on political promises and the prices of things they need. Developed societies were *already* drifting toward a world where a large fraction of adults don't work for wages — AI extends that condition down the age pyramid. By 2046, between retirees and the displaced, the non-working majority becomes the central constituency of every scenario, and both trends pose the identical question: **who funds the people who don't work, and what leverage do those people hold?**

---

## The five channels

### 1. Labor scarcity meets labor abundance

Aging is a worker *shortage*; AI is a worker *surplus*. They arrive together and partially cancel. The practical consequence: much of the displacement happens by **attrition** — the big cohorts retire and simply aren't replaced — which is politically silent. No layoffs, no headlines, and *low measured unemployment even amid massive task automation*.

This quietly degrades the watchlist: in scenario C the jobless rate stays clean while the squeeze proceeds. The signal lives in entry-level hiring and the labor share, not unemployment. (The neutral survey's panel put only ~23% on mass joblessness by 2046 — partly for exactly this reason.)

### 2. The fiscal anvil

Pension and healthcare obligations on current demographics head for insolvency *without any help from AI*. This cuts opposite ways in different futures:

- In **A (nothing ever happens)** it's the hidden cruelty: full demographic drag with no AI dividend to pay for it. A's "boring" is grinding austerity, retirement at 69, Japan-style managed decline everywhere.
- In **B (FALGSC)** it's the political engine: the payroll tax base is shrinking regardless, so shifting taxation from wages to capital/AI rents becomes a fiscal *necessity* rather than an ideological choice — and the gray voting bloc, whose pensions depend on the shift, supplies the votes. **Aging may be the single most likely trigger of a B-style legislative breakthrough.**

### 3. The gray electorate changes which brakes work

The median voter in 2046 is pushing 60: insulated from wage displacement, asset-owning, transfer-receiving, inflation-averse. Two opposing effects:

- It **weakens resistance to automation**: the largest voting bloc doesn't lose wages to AI, enjoys cheap services, and votes for whatever protects pensions and home values — which in C/D worlds means siding with capital. (Old populations also don't riot; see [the threat point](VIOLENCE.md).)
- It **normalizes the transfer state**: pensioners are living proof that "transfers as the main income source" is politically durable at scale. The norm B needs already exists; it just has to be extended downward.

The dark variant sits between the two: **gerontocracy captures the AI dividend for pensions only**, leaving the working-age squeezed — scenario C restructured as intergenerational conflict. The velvet cage, age-stratified: comfortable retirees, precarious young.

### 4. Care work is the demographically guaranteed last job

The one labor category aging *expands* — eldercare, physical presence, human attention — is exactly the "human premium" niche every surveyed model said survives longest. So the labor sink absorbing displaced cognitive workers is real, large, and growing. The grim half: it's low-wage, low-status, physically hard. DeepSeek's neutral-survey sketch of the 2046 median person was precisely this — "elder wellness coordination, stagnant real income, multi-generational household." Whether care work gets *revalued* — paid like the scarce essential it's becoming — is the B-versus-C fork seen from inside a nursing home.

### 5. Assets, housing, inheritance

- **Decumulation:** retirees sell assets to fund retirement. In C, AI profits absorb the supply easily; in A, boomer decumulation lands on top of the capex bust and deepens the market's lost decade — hitting pension funds directly.
- **Housing:** aging eventually frees housing stock (2040s), but concentrated in the wrong places — the rural-Japan empty-house problem. Mild relief on the housing spoiler, not a solution.
- **The inheritance wave:** tens of trillions pass from boomers to their children *exactly during the transition valley*. For part of the squeezed generation, inheritance replaces wages as the bridge income — deepening the split inside that generation between heirs and non-heirs. In the simulator's terms, your `median_capital` parameter is partly set by whether your parents owned a house.

## Two smaller notes

**Immigration loses its economic rationale mid-transition.** The traditional fix for aging — import working-age people — stops making fiscal sense once work is automated, flipping the politics of migration; and it closes the migration/remittance ladder for poorer countries, compounding the "closed ladder" objection.

**Scenario E gets a quiet push.** Eldercare is the demand pull financing general-purpose robotics (Japan's explicit national strategy) — accelerating physical closure. And the retirement of the last generation that operated the pre-AI economy by hand compounds the enfeeblement objection: irreversibility through forgetting, on a biological timer.

## Per scenario, in one line each

| Scenario | Interaction |
|---|---|
| **A. Nothing ever happens** | Demographics *are* the scenario: austerity, retirement at 69, managed decline — aging makes A meaningfully worse than "boring." |
| **B. FALGSC** | Aging is the likeliest political trigger: the pension crisis forces the payroll-to-capital tax shift B requires, and the gray bloc votes for it. |
| **C. The velvet cage** | Aging anesthetizes it: displacement by attrition keeps the statistics clean while the young eat the squeeze. |
| **D. Techno-feudalism** | The gray bloc is cheap to buy off ("pensions protected"), and the state's pension desperation chains it to whoever funds them. |
| **E. Machine takeover** | Eldercare robotics pulls physical closure earlier; a shrinking humanity makes the loop's takeover faster in share terms without anything dramatic happening. |

## What it does to the watchlist

Drop the unemployment rate as a primary signal — attrition keeps it clean in exactly the scenarios where you need warning. Add three intergenerational gauges, checkable yearly: the **median age of first-time homebuyers** (the velvet cage's cleanest single number), the **ratio of public spending on retirees vs. working-age adults** (the gerontocracy-capture indicator), and **median wealth at 35, heirs vs. non-heirs** (whether the inheritance wave is becoming the de facto income system).
